Was the future predictable? (forecasting backtest)

An experiment in open-endedness applied to itself (chart above): for each cutoff year Y we train a Model of Interestingness on papers up to Y, have an LLM constrained to a year-Y horizon generate “what comes next,” steer it toward the novel-but-learnable frontier with an evolutionary quality-diversity loop, then score those predictions against the papers that actually appeared after Y. It contrasts a steered agent vs. an unsteered baseline, and an honest (genuine ≤Y-cutoff) model vs. an oracle (modern model — a leakage upper bound, since it already knows the future). A research probe, not a benchmark: per-year samples are small, so read the trend across cutoffs.
